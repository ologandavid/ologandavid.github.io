MIT ELO Dot's Sit to Stand

Partnered with VME and Prof. Dan Frey to design a portable, compactable sit-to-stand apparatus to help elderly client with her needs in a handicapped bathroom. The design adds less than 15 pounds to the frame of the chair, while fitting narrowly within its margins.

The user is secured to the sit-to-stand using a harness. The attached pulley system and rotating base moves the patient over the toilet for cleaning. Product was manufactured with the help of MIT D-LAB. Additionally, we partnered with MIT Sandbox to market and sell product to assistive care facilities and hospitals.

MIT UROP: Rohsenow Kendall Heat Transfer Laboratory (Summer 2019)

During my freshman summer, I fabricated a glass heat resistant enclosure for thermal energy storage experiments. The preexisting acrylic shielding was unable to handle the heat emitted by the Induction Heater and had begun melting. Instead, I designed an easily replacable, heat resistant enclosure, to better suit the needs of the device and out research. Performed experiments on various materials to determine latent heat properties for storing electric energy through TES.

While in the RK, I also designed and repaired lab specific lab specific equipment for HFI-5 Induction Heater, Cooling System



MIT Projx/MITERS: Electric Skateboard (Spring 2019)

Sponsored by MIT through Projx in the construction of an electric longboard. Designed adjustable motor mount, to secure motor to longboard trucks. I also designed 48T HTD5 Wheel Pulley, which was 3D printed out of ABS for strength. Manufactured mount components, using ½” 60-61 Aluminum and shop waterjet and mill. (Electronic Speed Controller) ESC used for motor control while parallel LiPo batteries were used to power the system. Longboard reached a recorded max speed of 28 mph.

Columbia University: Lamont Doherty Earth Observatory (Spring 2018)

Starting in my junior year of high school, I worked as a research assistant ath Columbia Unversity. I assisted in the creation of a Sequence Stratigraphy Algorithm to determine Seismic Risk in the Marmara Sea. The Python based model was then used to analyze Seismic Data from the region. Paper won 1st Place at Sigma Xi Student Research Showcase in the GeoSciences Division.
